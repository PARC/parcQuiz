Penguin
Supportive cultures that may lack the killer instinct needed to thrive longer term.
  • Penguins often have well-deployed innovation process and tools, but don’t seem to get good returns from those capabilities.
  • Somehow – pressure of short-term deadlines, sheer scale, poor communications, lack of local management support – people within the organization are not able to take full advantage of them.
  • You feel that your organization is too large to manage innovation effectively or in an agile way.
  • Innovation is widely encouraged, but unfocused, and not quite integrated with company strategy or vision.
Penguin Recommendations
  1. Identify a clear growth vision and strategy: one that forces clear choices, i.e., excludes some opportunities/areas of business
  2. Link key innovation programs to business unit goals and set clear priorities.
  3. Develop ways to measure innovation programs and track their progress at both business unit and company levels.
  4. Link middle- and senior-management compensation to innovation objectives, to ensure sufficient focus on launching and managing innovation projects.
